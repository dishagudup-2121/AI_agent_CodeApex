ENTERPRISE FINANCIAL COMPLIANCE POLICY
Document Version: 3.2
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Classification: Internal – Confidential

OVERVIEW
This document outlines the financial compliance and risk management policies for all transaction processing systems. It applies to all business units handling monetary transactions, customer accounts, and cross-border payments. The goal is to ensure regulatory adherence, fraud prevention, and operational integrity.

SECTION 1: ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML)

1.1 Any single transaction exceeding $10,000 must be flagged for manual compliance review. This applies to all transaction types including wire transfers, ACH, and card payments.

1.2 Transactions originating from or destined to sanctioned countries (North Korea, Iran, Syria, Cuba, Crimea) must be immediately blocked. No exceptions are permitted regardless of transaction amount.

1.3 If a customer conducts more than 5 transactions within a 24-hour period and the customer is not classified as a VIP or institutional account, the activity must be flagged for velocity analysis.

1.4 Any single transaction exceeding $50,000 must be escalated to the compliance officer and blocked pending manual approval, regardless of account type or customer status.

SECTION 2: ACCOUNT RISK MANAGEMENT

2.1 New accounts (less than 30 days old) are prohibited from executing transactions exceeding $10,000. Such transactions must be blocked automatically.

2.2 Dormant accounts (no activity for 180 days or more) that suddenly initiate a transaction exceeding $5,000 must be flagged for reactivation review and potential fraud investigation.

2.3 Accounts with a risk score exceeding 80 (on a 0-100 scale) must have all outgoing transactions blocked until a manual risk assessment is completed.

SECTION 3: CROSS-BORDER COMPLIANCE

3.1 All international transactions (where the sender country differs from the receiver country) exceeding $25,000 must be flagged for enhanced due diligence review.

3.2 Transactions involving currency conversion with a spread exceeding 5% above the mid-market rate must be flagged for pricing compliance review.

SECTION 4: OPERATIONAL CONTROLS

4.1 No single user may initiate more than $500,000 in cumulative transaction volume within a single calendar day. Any attempt to exceed this limit must be blocked.

4.2 Weekend and holiday transactions exceeding $15,000 must be held for next-business-day review before processing.

APPENDIX A: DEFINITIONS
- VIP Account: An account with verified annual revenue exceeding $10 million
- Sanctioned Countries: As defined by OFAC SDN list, updated quarterly
- Dormant Account: No debit or credit activity for 180 consecutive days
- Risk Score: Composite score derived from transaction patterns, account behavior, and external watchlist matches
